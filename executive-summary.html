Executive Summary

Isomera Studio

A Biotechnology Innovation Studio for Rebuilding America's Capacity to Create, Invent, and Manufacture

Mission

Isomera Studio is a nonprofit dedicated to rebuilding America's capacity to create, invent, and manufacture.

We believe that America's long-term scientific, economic, and national-security competitiveness depends not only on technological breakthroughs, but on its ability to cultivate future generations of builders, researchers, engineers, entrepreneurs, and creators capable of producing those breakthroughs.

Our mission is therefore twofold: to develop transformative technologies and to develop the people capable of creating them.

We pursue this mission through creative projects that combine scientific research, artificial intelligence, biotechnology, engineering, education, and public engagement.

Origins

The idea for Isomera Studio emerged while our founder was teaching herself to program and develop AI systems for molecular design.

Working with the molecular-generation software of the day, she noticed that most systems could optimize molecules mathematically but lacked an intuitive understanding of how molecular structures occupy space, move, and interact.

The observation led to an unexpected question. When artists learn to draw the human hand, they do not simply memorize its appearance—they develop an understanding of the underlying anatomy and mechanics through the act of creation. What if AI could learn molecular structure in a similar way?

Exploring that question led to the development of Fragment-Based Molecular Expansion (FRAME), an AI-driven approach to molecular generation that resulted in peer-reviewed publications, scientific presentations, and collaborations in therapeutic discovery.

More importantly, the experience revealed that breakthrough innovation often emerges at the intersection of disciplines. FRAME was not the product of biology, computer science, or art alone, but of combining insights from all three.

That realization became the foundation of Isomera Studio.

We believe that developing transformative technologies requires developing the people capable of thinking across boundaries and creating them. Isomera Studio was established to cultivate both—combining biotechnology research, artificial intelligence, workforce development, entrepreneurship, and public engagement into a single innovation ecosystem dedicated to rebuilding America's capacity to create, invent, and manufacture.

Biotechnology Innovation Studio

Isomera Studio proposes a model we refer to as the Biotechnology Innovation Studio.

The model is founded on the belief that scientific discovery, engineering, entrepreneurship, education, and creative practice are not separate activities, but different expressions of the same fundamental process: understanding the world through creation.

This perspective emerged from our founder's work in artificial intelligence for molecular design, where insights from art, biology, and computation combined to produce new approaches to therapeutic discovery. It revealed that breakthrough innovation often occurs not within disciplines, but at their intersections.

The Biotechnology Innovation Studio therefore integrates four functions that are traditionally separated across different organizations:

  • AI-enabled biotechnology research and development
  • Technology translation and venture creation
  • Workforce development and technical training
  • Public engagement and science communication

Rather than treating these activities as distinct programs, we view them as complementary expressions of a single innovation ecosystem. Scientific research generates new knowledge. Entrepreneurship transforms knowledge into capability. Education develops the people capable of advancing it. Public engagement creates the shared understanding necessary for innovation to flourish within society.

Through this integrated approach, Isomera Studio seeks to develop both transformative technologies and the human capacity required to create, understand, and sustain them.

AI-Driven Therapeutic Discovery

One of Isomera Studio's primary research programs focuses on artificial intelligence for molecular generation and therapeutic discovery.

A key example is our collaboration with Chromologic. Together, we are combining Isomera Studio's molecular generation platform with Chromologic's expertise in drug delivery and translational biotechnology.

The collaboration focuses on identifying compounds capable of protecting the brain from traumatic injury before irreversible damage occurs. Current interventions for traumatic brain injury largely address damage after injury has already taken place. Our objective is to identify compounds capable of intervening earlier in the injury cascade, reducing or preventing the cellular and molecular damage that leads to long-term neurological impairment.

This effort has particular relevance for military personnel, veterans, and others at elevated risk of traumatic brain injury.

To support this work, we have established a mouse model of concussive traumatic brain injury that provides a platform for evaluating candidate compounds in vivo. This capability allows us to move beyond computational discovery and assess whether newly identified molecules can reduce neurological damage and improve outcomes following injury.

We are also exploring applications in neuropsychiatric disorders, molecular glue discovery, and other areas where advances in artificial intelligence may significantly accelerate therapeutic development.

Public Engagement and Science Communication

The fourth pillar of the Biotechnology Innovation Studio is public engagement and science communication.

We believe that scientific innovation is ultimately a public endeavor. Society invests significant resources in research through public institutions, philanthropy, and private investment, yet many people experience science as something distant from their daily lives. They may benefit from new discoveries without ever understanding the problems researchers are trying to solve, the motivations that drive scientific work, or the human stories behind technological progress.

For this reason, we view public engagement not as an auxiliary activity, but as a core component of innovation itself. Institutions have a responsibility not only to create new knowledge, but also to create meaningful ways for people to connect with that knowledge and understand why it matters.

Projects such as Ragnarök: Severance of the Body from the Mind embody this philosophy. The installation explores mental illness, traumatic brain injury, consciousness, and healing through the integration of art, science, and community participation. Built from reclaimed windows and doors assembled into fractal geometric structures, the work transforms scientific ideas into physical experiences that people can see, contemplate, and emotionally connect with.

On each pane is a hand-drawn molecular structure derived from a medicinal substance. Some were first discovered through traditional healing practices and medicines passed down across generations; others were developed through modern scientific research. Together, they represent a continuous human effort spanning cultures, centuries, and disciplines to understand the mind, alleviate suffering, and develop new tools for healing. Behind each molecular drawing is an infinity-mirror chamber representing consciousness itself—an attempt to visualize the vast, layered, and often hidden nature of the human mind.

By giving physical form to these compounds, the installation transforms ideas that are normally confined to laboratories, scientific papers, and textbooks into something people can experience directly. The goal is not simply to explain the science, but to help people understand the deeper motivations behind it: humanity's desire to reduce suffering, expand knowledge, and shape its own future. Through projects such as Ragnarök, Isomera Studio seeks to build stronger connections between scientific research and the communities it serves, ensuring that innovation remains not only technologically transformative, but also culturally meaningful and broadly understood.

Long-Term Vision

Isomera Studio seeks to establish a new kind of institution: part accelerator, part research laboratory, part design studio, part workshop, and part educational organization.

Our objective is not only to accelerate biotechnology innovation but to rebuild the broader culture of invention that makes innovation possible. By combining scientific discovery, entrepreneurship, workforce development, and public engagement into a single ecosystem, we aim to create both technological breakthroughs and the people needed to create them.

We believe America's future competitiveness will depend not only on what technologies it invents, but on how effectively it develops the next generation of builders capable of inventing them.

Our goal is not simply to invent new technologies, but to help create a society capable of continually inventing its future.
